Began his artistic career in 2005 after graduating from the Far Eastern State Academy of Arts in Vladivostok (Russia) as an actor of dramatic theater and cinema.

In 2005 - 2008 lived in Moscow and St. Petersburg, working as a theater and film actor, as well as trying himself in theater directing.

Over the years, has starred in more than 20 motion pictures and theatrical projects where he participated as an actor, as well as staging some of his own drama productions.

In 2008 he became a visiting member of several projects of physical theatre company DEREVO (Dresden, Germany), through which he first experiences the style butoh.

In 2009, creates his own theater and dance company PoemaTheatre (St.-Petersburg - Moscow), with which he begins developing a new method called "the semiotics of human body". The method was formed under the influence of the French philosophy of deconstruction of the 60s.

Through the course of 2009 to 2015, together with PoemaTheatre, Valentin creates about 100 different projects (stage productions, as well as site specific improvisations). And on the basis of "the semiotics of human body" their own method was carried out a large number of master classes and shows of solo projects and collaborations with other artists in Russia, Ukraine, Germany, the Netherlands, France, Hungary, Athens, Italy, Spain, Portugal, Colombia, Thailand, Taiwan and Japan..

In 2011, Valentin creates the research network project Butoh Channel, which argues the relevance of the philosophy of art of butoh in the context of contemporary cultural and political issues. The project has collaborated with such prominent artists as the butoh company Dairakudakan, Ko Murobushi, Carlotta Ikeda, Flavia Ghisalberti, Masaki Iwana, overtone singer Sainkho Namtchylak and many others.

In 2015 Valentin moved to Berlin ( head of PoemaTheatre Moscow, Evgenia Chetvertkova, becomes the company's main choreographer). Under the influence of modern philosophy of Speculative Realism Valentin develops a new method :"Body Alienation" with which he enters the master's program SODA (HZT-Berlin) at the UDK-Berlin. The main research question: dance not only as a tool of transformation of body and personality, but also the utopian idea of ​​dance as a change in the future (and personality patterns of DNA changes). The main areas are the techno culture experiments and parametric architecture (project "out of the spectacle" in architectural objects of Zaha Hadid).
In 2016, Valentin is forced to leave the Masters and concentrate on our own experiments, as well as the creation of new schools and later dance company in Berlin.
