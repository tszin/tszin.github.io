BUTOH
Butoh first appeared in Japan post-World War II in 1959, under the collaboration of Hijikata Tatsumi and Ohno Kazuo, "in the protective shadow of the 1950s and 1960s avant-garde". A key impetus of the art form was a reaction against the capitalistic invasion and Japanese dance scene then, which Hijikata felt was overly based on imitating the West and following traditional styles like Noh. Thus, he sought to "turn away from the Western styles of dance, ballet and modern", and to create a new aesthetic that embraced the "squat, earthbound physique... and the natural movements of the common folk" This desire found form in the early movement of ankoku butō (暗黒舞踏), «Dance of Darkness»

4/4 4/3 4/0 CONCEPT
A Concept of Three Contradictions of Body movement 4/4 4/3 4/0 has been developed by Valentin Tszin during almost 10 years, based on sports, theatre, and dance techniques.
4/4: Adoption (classical and native dance, discotheque) 4/3: Contradiction (rhythmics, biomechanics, clown and physical theatre), 4/0: Renouncement (techno, parametricism, butoh)
WORKSHOP
Consists of four basic components: the body warm-up, work with a rhythm, body transformation and creation of the happening.
The participants should wear convenient sportive clothes, have notebook and pen. Please do not use perfume. Classes will be held barefoot.
Level of participants: physically fit shape is preferred.
VALENTIN TSZIN
In 2005 he graduated from the theatre department of the Far Eastern State Academy of Arts / FEAA and since then has gone through a long series of artistic transformation as an actor of dramatic theatre, film actor, performer of physical theatre, butoh dancer, throat singer.
Also continues teaching based on his own methodology, which he began to develop in 2009, creating his own project theater and dance group PoemaTheatre (Russia).
Valentin’s performances, workshops and collaborations with various artists were held in Eastern and Western Europe, South America and Asia.
Since 2015 he is based in Berlin, where continues his research based on his personal Concept of «4/4 4/3 4/0 Dance Art»
